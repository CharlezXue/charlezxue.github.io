## Photographer’s style
The photographer’s style can be described as Melancholic, Lo-fi, and Cinematic, with a strong influence from 90s/00s film photography and Asian "private photography" (shashin) aesthetics.
Here is a breakdown of the key elements defining this style:
1. Color Palette & Temperature
Dominant Cool Tones: The most striking feature is the heavy use of blue and cyan. Images 1, 2, and 3 are washed in deep, cold blues, evoking feelings of night, water, isolation, and dreaminess. This is often referred to as a "blue hour" aesthetic.
Stark Contrasts: In Image 4, the photographer breaks the blue monotony with a jarring contrast of clinical white and visceral red (blood), creating a sense of shock or intensity.
Monochrome: Image 5 utilizes high-contrast black and white, stripping away color to focus on texture and emotion.
2. Lighting & Atmosphere
Low-Light & Ambient Light: The photos rely heavily on available light (streetlights, window light, moonlight). This creates a moody, intimate atmosphere where shadows are deep and details are sometimes lost, adding to the mystery.
Hard Flash: Image 5 uses a direct, on-camera flash. This creates a "snapshot" look—harsh shadows and blown-out highlights—that feels raw, immediate, and unpolished.
Ethereal Glow: In the water photo (Image 1), the light seems to diffuse through the liquid, giving the subject a ghostly, ethereal appearance.
3. Texture & Quality
High Grain/Noise: All images possess a significant amount of grain. This mimics the look of high-ISO film stock. It adds a tactile, gritty texture that makes the photos feel nostalgic and analog, even if taken digitally.
Soft Focus/Blur: Images 2 and 3 have a slight motion blur or softness, suggesting movement or a fleeting moment captured in time. It feels candid rather than posed.
4. Mood & Narrative
Introspection & Loneliness: The subjects are often alone, looking away, or in contemplative poses (staring out a window, floating in water). It evokes a sense of urban isolation and youthful angst.
Visceral & Dark: The inclusion of the blood in the basin (Image 4) and the submerged figure (Image 1) introduces a darker, slightly disturbing undertone. It moves beyond simple portraiture into something more psychological or narrative-driven.
Cinematic: The framing, especially the shots by the window (Images 2 & 3), feels like stills from an indie movie or a Wong Kar-wai film (specifically Fallen Angels or Chungking Express), emphasizing mood over plot.
Summary
This photographer creates atmospheric, emotion-driven imagery. They prioritize mood and texture over technical perfection. The style is a blend of nostalgic film aesthetics and modern "sad-core" or "dreamcore" visuals, focusing on themes of youth, isolation, and the beauty found in darker or quieter moments.

## Website style guide

The website exhibits a distinct Y2K / Retro-Futuristic aesthetic mixed with surreal digital art elements. Here is a breakdown of its visual style:
Dreamy Gradient Background: The backdrop features a soft, airbrushed gradient that transitions from a warm peach/salmon color at the top, through a sky blue, down to a grassy green at the bottom. This creates a surreal, horizon-like effect.
3D "Puffy" Typography: The central focal point is the large Chinese text, which is rendered in a bright orange-red 3D style. It resembles puffy paint, inflated balloons, or clay, giving it a tactile, playful appearance.
Lo-Fi Texture & Grain: The entire image has a heavy "noise" or film grain overlay. This gives the website a gritty, analog feel, reminiscent of old VHS tapes, scanned film photography, or early 2000s digital art.
Surreal Elements: There are floating, out-of-focus spheres or bubbles scattered across the "sky," adding a sense of depth and a dreamlike, almost space-like atmosphere.
Vibrant & Playful Color Palette: The design uses high-contrast, saturated colors. The bright yellow subtitle ("Craving for some spice?") pops against the blue/green background, while the red navigation text ties in with the central logo.
Bilingual Layout: The interface seamlessly blends English and Simplified Chinese, suggesting a modern, cosmopolitan brand identity (likely a trendy restaurant).
Overall, the style is nostalgic, artistic, and bold, moving away from clean corporate minimalism in favor of something more expressive and emotionally evocative.
